The thesis, applied to Risk Copilot.

Eduba works one layer above the tools. Our thesis: sixty percent of most problems is traditional code and database work, thirty percent is rule-based logic, ten percent is genuine AI. Applied to Risk Copilot, the catalog of 300,000 parameterized risks is a lookup problem. The control-to-evidence mapping is rules. The regulatory explanation and gap narrative is model work. Separating them cuts inference spend, tightens accuracy, and gives Gabriela's team a testable spec for every jurisdiction the platform enters next.

The closest engagement in our library.

VigilOre, a multi-agent compliance platform for mining operators in the DRC. We compressed 160+ hours of monthly compliance review into under five minutes by organizing their regulatory context as structured, interpretable layers and putting a small agent network on top. The structure is public: our Interpretable Context Methodology paper, submitted to ACM TiiS, describes how agent context becomes a layered filesystem with measurable interpretability gains. For a team adding jurisdictions on a quarterly cadence, this is the pattern.
